## Key extracts
**Cost (reconciled ranges)**: headline 20–50% savings = factory-cost-only comparisons; realistic all-in net ~10%; multi-unit PBR up to 20% + reduced overrun risk. Ontario benchmarks: $100–$250/sq ft factory-only; $200–$450/sq ft all-in turnkey.
**Speed**: 30–50% faster (Modular Building Institute), up to 2–3x on some multi-unit. Cases: Pine Valley Lodge (Hinton AB, 100-bed, 5 months faster); 605 Studio West (Calgary, 84 units, <1 yr vs ~2); Fuze Lucan (Lucan ON, 90 units, Peakhill + Z Modular).
**Financing unlock (2026)**:
- CMHC extended MLI Select to modular **May 7, 2026** (after 800+ unit pilot across 5 provinces) — closes the factory-phase draw mismatch that historically killed modular loans
- CMHC Prefab Plus: 5% down, staged fund release (homeownership side)
- Precedent: Peakhill + Z Modular $26M — first CMHC-insured modular apartment construction loan in Canada
**Policy**: Build Canada Homes launched Sep 14, 2025, $13B capitalization; **$1B equity + $25B debt earmarked for prefab builders**; 6 federal Direct Build sites targeting 4,000 prefab homes from 2026; modular ~40%+ GHG reduction (supports MLI Select energy scoring)
**Constraints**: ~40 Canadian manufacturers, most <100 units/yr; municipal zoning fragmentation erodes repeat-order economics; developer still fronts factory-phase cash (int'l insolvency precedents: Ilke Homes, Modulous, Urban Splash)
**BC relevance conclusion**: strongest case for the Sapperton file is the *financing* story (MLI Select now covers modular) + carry-cost reduction from speed, not raw cost savings. Confirm BC manufacturer capacity/lead time before underwriting timeline benefits.
