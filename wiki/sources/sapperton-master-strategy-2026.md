## Key extracts

### Demand anchor
- RCH: 3,300+ employees (2017 baseline); 612+ open postings on ZipRecruiter alone (Jun 2026); Jim Pattison Tower adds ~50% more beds, opened May 31, 2026; $1.5B investment
- Campus: 607,859 sq ft, L-shaped across E Columbia/Sherbrooke/Allen/Keary/Brunette
- Brewery District (5-min walk): TransLink HQ, Transit Police HQ, Fraser Health HQ, HSABC HQ

### 3-phase plan
1. **Proof of Concept (2026–2028)**: acquire 419+407, 6-unit Infill Program application, market as healthcare-worker rental, Fraser Health partnership letter, CMHC ACLP + MLI Select. 6–12 units.
2. **Corridor Assembly (2027–2030)**: +3–5 lots → 15,000–25,000 sq ft, mid-rise rezoning post-2027 design guidelines, 8-storey/3.0 FSR 100% secured rental, AHF approach, Fraser Health anchor lease, institutional capital. 80–150 units.
3. **District Vision (2029–2034)**: 30–60-lot land study, bespoke city "Sapperton Health Housing District" designation, 3–5 buildings, mixed tenure (rental + co-op + below-market), clinic/childcare ground floors, Province/Fraser Health co-investment. 300–500+ units.

### Phase 2 capital stack (illustrative)
1. CMHC ACLP → MLI Select takeout: up to 100% LTC, ~$18M–$22M; 95% LTV / 50-yr am; DSCR ~1.10–1.20x
2. AHF/Build Canada Homes grant: $2M–$5M forgivable ($1.5B top-up announced Sep 2025; 20+ yr affordability covenant)
3. Fraser Health master lease: 30–50 units, ~$800K–$1.5M/yr, 10-yr commitment
4. Sponsor equity + institutional co-invest: 15–20% (~$3M–$6M)

### Policy hooks
- OCP **Special Employment Area** within 5-min walk of RCH
- NW Interim Housing Needs Report: **8,137 new units by 2029**
- Federal **Housing Accelerator Fund deadline June 15, 2026** — city incentivized to approve shovel-ready housing
- Council's explicit preference for 100% secured rental in TOA rezonings
- Provincial nurse/healthcare-worker shortage; RCH is UBC teaching hospital

### Risk register (severity / mitigant)
- Assembly fails (moderate): two lots market-listed; option agreements funded by Phase 1 cash flow
- Council election Oct 2026 (moderate): file Phase 1 before election; HAF + provincial TOA mandates constrain reversal
- Fraser Health won't sign (moderate): not needed for Phase 1; demand proven by postings
- CMHC slow/fails (moderate): 4–6 mo processing; conventional bridge fallback
- Cost inflation (moderate): offset by 100% LTC, 50-yr am, AHF; model to positive cash flow at 85% occupancy
- Community opposition (low): "healthcare worker housing is the single most community-acceptable form of density in Sapperton"; SRA pragmatic
