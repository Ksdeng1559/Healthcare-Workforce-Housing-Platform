## Key extracts

### Site (Section 1–2)
- 419 & 407 Garrett St: two contiguous **RS-1** lots, ~10,440 sq ft combined, ~90 ft combined frontage
- Inside New Westminster's designated Transit-Oriented Area, ~400–800m from Sapperton SkyTrain Station (**Tier 3** — to be confirmed with City Planning before binding acquisition)
- 3-min walk from Fraser Health head office (Brewery District)

### Three density pathways (Section 2)
| Metric | A — RS-1 today | B — Infill Program (adopted Jun 2026) | C — TOA mid-rise (rezoning) |
|---|---|---|---|

Cited: NW Zoning Bylaw No. 6680 (2001); TOA Designation Bylaw No. 8460 (adopted Jun 2024); OCP & Zoning Amendment Bylaws No. 8573/8578 (Infill Housing Program — third reading May 25, 2026, adopted Jun 2026). Land values are Eng & Associates estimates.

### Demand anchor (Section 3)
- RCH: BC's only SkyTrain-adjacent Level 1 trauma centre; 607,859 sq ft campus; UBC Faculty of Medicine affiliate
- $1.5B three-phase redevelopment; Jim Pattison Acute Care Tower opened May 31, 2026 — 388 beds, 47-bed ICU, 48-bed obstetrics + NICU, 75-space ED, 17 ORs; $30M gift from Jim Pattison
- 600+ active RCH postings (Jun 2026) vs 3,300+ employee baseline (2017)
- Fraser Health: 29,000–50,000 staff, 1.9M+ residents served; HQ + HSABC HQ at Brewery District

### VGH precedent (Section 3)
- Harrison Street AM + PC Urban, announced Mar 31, 2026: C$265M, 507 units, twin 22-storey towers at W 13th & Willow across from VGH; furnished, shift-based leasing, 11,000 sq ft childcare; nomination agreement gives VGH employees priority below-market access; groundbreaking Aug 2026, delivery Jun 2029
- Quote: "There wasn't an existing policy framework for this kind of housing… this could serve as a precedent" — Brent Sawchyn, CEO, PC Urban
- Comparison: Sapperton land $430–$530/sq ft vs Fairview $700–$1,000/sq ft; 15–30+ RS-1 lots within 500m vs extremely limited

### Financing stack (Section 4)
- [[cmhc-aclp]]: construction financing up to 100% LTC (post-Nov 2024 reforms), interest-only during build
- [[mli-select]]: takeout up to 95% LTV, up to 50-yr amortization, tiered by points (50/70/100 — affordability/energy/accessibility); "model to mid-tier"
- [[affordable-housing-fund]] (under Build Canada Homes): forgivable contributions for below-market units to income-qualified tenants (e.g., healthcare workers under Fraser Health income thresholds)

### Residual land value method (Section 4)
Assumes 3.0 FSR, 85% net saleable efficiency, $950/sq ft GDV, $375/sq ft hard, 15% developer margin. Comp: Cedar Court + Griffiths Manor TOA listing — 41,382 sq ft at $21M ($507/sq ft, Goodman Commercial, Oct 2024).

### Engagement structure + risks (Section 5)
- Steps: acquire ($3.6M–$4.2M target) → Phase 1 infill DP + stabilize → Fraser Health master-lease MOU (30–50 units) + CMHC ACLP pre-qual → JV with QuadReal/BCI, Wesgroup, Harrison Street, or HOOPP → scale to 15,000–25,000 sq ft parcel, then district 300–500+ units
- Risks: 407 already market-listed with TOA language; 419 not listed (direct-approach opportunity); TOA tier unconfirmed; CMHC processing 4–6 months; NW council election Oct 2026 (file before election)
- No listed party has committed capital as of memo date
