# Community Sentiment & Approval Risk — Sapperton
**Last updated**: 2026-07-07
**Key entities**: [[sapperton-residents-association]], [[new-westminster]], [[sapperton]], [[garrett-407-419-assembly]]
**Open questions**: Oct 2026 council election outcome; water/sewer servicing capacity on Garrett St

## Summary
One analyst report (May 2026, Dennis Soh / ClearClose — the set's only non-Eng-branded document) rates Sapperton "upper quartile of Metro Vancouver for development receptivity": pragmatic residents' association, pro-housing but design-cautious council, validated absorption. Approval risk is moderate (5.5/10) and concentrated in *form and tenure*, not density itself.

## Evidence
- **Community**: SRA is "conditional YIMBY" — objects via traffic/livability, never density per se; coded signals: 72% TOD-density acceptance, 15% rental opposition, 80% traffic concern (analyst interpretation, not polling)
- **Council**: approved Columbia Square (3,800 units/53 storeys, 5-2), all-station TOA bylaws, Infill Program (3,000 properties), Townhouse Accelerator (900 properties); but rejected Lower Twelfth heights as "excessive" and delayed mid-rise design guidelines to early 2027; mayor: "Resistance is futile"
- **Absorption**: Brewery District (750+ homes, retail vacancy <2%), Braid Station rental leasing, Illuma townhomes sold, 407 Garrett listed with TOA language — market has priced the uplift down to street level
- **Application guidance**: works = infill 4–6 units now, 100% rental mid-rise, articulated design, early SRA engagement, healthcare narrative ("the single most community-acceptable form of density in Sapperton"). Hard blockers = market-strata rezoning, "mini apartment block" form, servicing deficiencies.
- **Comparatives**: more receptive than Vancouver West Side, Lynn Valley (petition history), Surrey (townhouse rejection); comparable to Burnaby

## Tensions and uncertainties
- Sentiment percentages are analyst-coded, not surveyed; sole source, author's relationship to sponsor unknown
- Design-guideline gap until early 2027 penalizes early mid-rise applications — pushes Phase 2 timing
- Aging water/sewer may require developer-funded upgrades — unquantified cost risk absent from the pro forma
