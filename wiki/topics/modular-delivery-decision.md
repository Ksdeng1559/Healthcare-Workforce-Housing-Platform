# Modular Delivery Decision
**Last updated**: 2026-07-07
**Key entities**: [[modular-construction]], [[mli-select]], [[build-canada-homes]], [[sapperton-district-project]]
**Open questions**: BC manufacturer capacity/lead times; modular economics at 6-unit Phase 1 scale

## Summary
Modular is a live option, not a commitment: platform policy treats it as "preferred where it improves timing, financing, cost certainty and sustainability," and the July 2026 research brief concludes the real 2026 unlock is *financing* (CMHC MLI Select extended to modular May 7, 2026), not the oversold cost story.

## Evidence
- Realistic all-in savings ~10% (up to 20% on repeat multi-unit orders); the 20–50% headlines compare factory cost only [modular-housing-brief-2026]
- Timeline compression 30–50% is the better-documented benefit → shorter construction-loan carry, earlier stabilized income — directly underwriting-relevant on a CMHC ACLP/MLI Select file
- Precedent: Peakhill + Z Modular $26M Fuze Lucan loan — first CMHC-insured modular apartment construction loan in Canada; CMHC pilot insured 800+ modular rentals before the May 2026 expansion
- Policy: Build Canada Homes earmarks $1B equity + $25B debt for prefab builders; modular's ~40% GHG reduction supports MLI Select energy-efficiency points (synergy with the green rental strategy [platform-repo-docs])
- Constraints: ~40 Canadian manufacturers mostly <100 units/yr; developer still fronts factory-phase cash; municipal design variation erodes repeat-order economics

## Decision frame for Sapperton
Phase 1 (6 units): modular unlikely to pencil — savings scale with repetition; confirm before assuming. Phase 2+ (80–150 repeatable rental units): strongest case — repeat orders, MLI Select-insured financing, energy points, carry savings. Gate: confirm a BC-based/serving manufacturer's capacity and lead time first.

## Tensions and uncertainties
- All figures secondary-source (Peakhill-heavy) — verify against CMHC primary docs + manufacturer quotes before underwriting
