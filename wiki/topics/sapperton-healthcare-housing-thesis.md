# The Sapperton Healthcare Housing Thesis
**Last updated**: 2026-07-07
**Key entities**: [[sapperton-district-project]], [[garrett-407-419-assembly]], [[royal-columbian-hospital]], [[fraser-health]], [[vgh-workforce-housing]], [[cmhc-aclp]], [[mli-select]], [[affordable-housing-fund]]
**Open questions**: TOA tier confirmation; Fraser Health engagement status; resolution of the $1.85M vs $3.6M–$4.2M land figure; independent verification of all sponsor estimates

## Summary
The platform's core thesis: assemble RS-1 land near Royal Columbian Hospital at infill pricing, entitle it under New Westminster's adopted TOA and Infill Housing frameworks, and build purpose-built rental for RCH's healthcare workforce — financed on CMHC's rental stack and de-risked by the Harrison Street/PC Urban VGH precedent. All economics are sponsor (Eng & Associates) pre-feasibility estimates; nothing independently verified as of Jul 2026.

## Evidence
**Demand**: RCH is completing a $1.5B expansion; the Jim Pattison Tower (388 beds, +~50% capacity) opened May 31, 2026, with 600+ open postings against a 3,300+ (2017) baseline. Demand is institutional and non-cyclical — "underwrite to a hospital, not a housing cycle" [sapperton-investment-memorandum-2026; sapperton-master-strategy-2026 — two sponsor documents, shared authorship, so effectively one source chain citing Fraser Health and Province releases].

**Policy**: Density near Sapperton Station is compelled, not merely permitted: TOA Bylaw 8460 (Jun 2024, 8 storeys/3.0 FSR min at Tier 3) plus the Infill Housing Program (adopted Jun 2026, 4–6 units by DP only). Council prefers 100% secured rental in TOA rezonings; the city owes 8,137 units by 2029 [sapperton-investment-memorandum-2026].

**Precedent**: VGH workforce housing (announced Mar 2026, C$265M, 507 units) proves financeability and political approval — in a harder land/policy environment than Sapperton (land $700–$1,000/sq ft vs $430–$530; no pre-existing framework vs adopted TOA) [both 2026 sources, citing press releases].

**Financing**: ACLP (≤100% LTC) → MLI Select (≤95% LTV, 50-yr am) + AHF forgivable grants + proposed Fraser Health master lease (30–50 units). Phase 1 TPC $3.76M for 6 units, executable now [sapperton-investment-memorandum-2026].

**Evolution**: This thesis supersedes the Dec 2024 Garrett Street proposal (6-lot strata concept, $63M–$80M revenue) — scope narrowed to 419+407, model flipped from condo sales to rental hold, zoning corrected from "RD" to RS-1 [see [[garrett-street-project]] supersession].

## Tensions and uncertainties
- **Phase 1 value gap**: stabilized income value ($2.8M–$3.8M) < total project cost ($3.76M) at midpoints — Phase 1 is a land-appreciation and proof-of-concept play, not a yield play. Memo doesn't state this plainly.
- ~~Land basis gap~~ resolved: pro forma's $1.85M is a "(share of purchase)" allocation [developer-presentation-2026, slide 8].
- **Entitlement messaging gap**: internal docs = TOA mid-rise needs rezoning + hearing (guidelines 2027); owner-facing landing page = "as-of-right, no public hearing, 3–5 months." See [[rlm-designation-nw]] — unresolved, and a stakeholder-credibility risk if the page goes live unreconciled.
- **Evidence base**: 11 of 12 ingested sources are sponsor-produced; the citations methodology doc [investor-citations-methodology-2026] substantially upgrades sourcing for investor/RCH/VGH claims (primary URLs), but no independent appraisal, QS report, or city confirmation exists in wiki. The one non-Eng document (sentiment analysis, ClearClose) has unknown independence.
- **Execution status (Jul 7, 2026)**: everything is pre-outreach — no site control, no TOA confirmation, no Fraser Health contact, no capital committed. The thesis is fully documented and fully unexecuted.
- 2017 employment baseline (3,326) is stale by 9 years.
