**Type**: Concept (delivery method)

## What we know
[modular-housing-brief-2026 — well-referenced secondary synthesis]:
- **Cost**: realistic all-in savings ~10% (headline 20–50% = factory-cost-only); multi-unit PBR up to 20% + lower overrun risk. Ontario: $100–$250/sq ft factory-only, $200–$450/sq ft turnkey
- **Speed**: 30–50% faster (concurrent factory + site work); cases: 605 Studio West Calgary (84 units, <1 yr), Pine Valley Lodge (5 mo faster), Fuze Lucan (90 units)
- **Financing unlocked 2026**: CMHC extended [[mli-select]] to modular May 7, 2026; Prefab Plus (5% down, staged draws); Peakhill+Z Modular $26M = first CMHC-insured modular apartment construction loan in Canada
- **Policy tailwind**: [[build-canada-homes]] earmarks $1B equity + $25B debt for prefab builders; 6 federal Direct Build sites, 4,000 prefab homes from 2026; ~40%+ GHG reduction supports MLI Select energy scoring
- **Constraints**: ~40 Canadian manufacturers, mostly <100 units/yr; zoning fragmentation erodes repeat-order economics; developer fronts factory-phase cash (Ilke/Modulous/Urban Splash insolvency precedents)

## Application to platform
Platform stance: "preferred option where it improves timing, financing, cost certainty and sustainability… not mandatory until site and supplier feasibility confirmed" [platform-repo-docs]. Brief's conclusion for the Sapperton file: the case is the *financing + carry-cost* story, not raw cost; confirm BC manufacturer capacity first.
